what is normalisation?

Normalization is the process of adjusting the wavefunction Ψ(x) so that the integral of its squared modulus over all space equals 1:
−∞∫∞ |Ψ(x)|2.dx = 1
Here, |Ψ(x)|2represents the probability density, which describes the likelihood of finding the particle at a specific position x.

-------- KEY POINTS --------

  • • If Ψ is not normalized, then:
    ∫ |Ψ(x)|2.dτ = A ≠ 1

  • • A corresponding normalized wavefunction (ΨNorm) can be defined:
    ΨNorm(r) = 1/√2 Ψ(r)
    such that:
    ∫ |ΨNorm(x)|2.dτ = 1

  • • The factor (1/√A) is known as the normalisation constant (sometimes represented by N).

-------- PROPERTIES OF ACCEPTABLE WAVEFUNCTION --------

  • • Ψ must be finite everywhere.

  • • Ψ must be single valued.

  • • Ψ and dΨ/dx must be continuous for finite well-behaved potentials V(x).

  • • Ψ must be normalizable (generally Ψ → 0 as x → inf).
